Problem & Solution

The sport is active, but the community feels scattered.

Fédération Equestre Romande (FER) supports equestrian sport in Suisse Romande. National Swiss Equestrian Federation (Swiss Equestrian) provides national calendars and event information.

📅

Fragmented information

Riders and fans often search across calendars, stable pages, social media posts and word of mouth to know what is happening.

🤝

Volunteer shortage

Horse shows need people for parking, bar, paddock, course crew, timing and hospitality, but recruitment is rarely centralized.

✨

Community energy

Parties, food stands and social evenings make events memorable, but this information is often hard to find before attending.

🔎

Mysterious sport

Show jumping has its own rhythm, codes and vocabulary. To newcomers, it can feel like a closed world; JumpLink makes it more welcoming, readable and easy to join.

The solution

A community-first event platform.

JumpLink Romandie presents show jumping events and adds the missing layers: volunteer sign-ups, sponsorship opportunities, social events and organizer visibility.

Show Jumping 101

Basic knowledge for newcomers.

What happens during a show jumping competition

1Riders have 15 minutes to walk the course and memorize the track.
2Horse and rider warm up in a ring called "training ring" where they share some warm up jumps, helped by their trainers.
3The horse and rider combination enters the main arena to complete the course.
4After jumping, the horses are walked, washed or brushed off so that they recover in optimal conditions.
5The best competitors come back in the main arena for the prize giving ceremony at the end of the class.
CS

Concours de Saut

In Switzerland, show jumping competitions are often marked as “CS”. They can include regional or national classes.

R / N

Competition levels

The horse shows are separated in different categories: National, Regional, Brevet and Pony.

How to win?

The goal is to be the fastest to complete the course, while leaving all the jumps up. Knocking a rail down, having your horse refuse a jump or completing your course slower than the time allowed, are all events that will lead to added penalties.
