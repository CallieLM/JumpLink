Problem & Solution

The sport is active, but the community feels scattered.

FER represents equestrian sport in Suisse Romande, while Swiss Equestrian provides national sport calendars and event information. JumpLink builds a bridge between official event data and everyday community needs.

📅

Fragmented information

Riders and fans often search across calendars, stable pages, social media posts and word of mouth to know what is happening.

🤝

Volunteer shortage

Horse shows need people for parking, bar, paddock, course crew, timing and hospitality, but recruitment is rarely centralized.

✨

Community energy

Parties, food stands and social evenings make events memorable, but this information is often hard to find before attending.

🔎

Mysterious sport

Show jumping has its own rhythm, codes and vocabulary. To newcomers, it can feel like a closed world; JumpLink makes it more welcoming, readable and easy to join.

The solution

A community-first event platform.

JumpLink Romandie presents show jumping events and adds the missing layers: volunteer sign-ups, sponsorship opportunities, social events and organizer visibility.

Show Jumping 101

Basic knowledge for newcomers.

The platform should also educate new people entering the sport, so they feel comfortable helping or attending.

What happens during a jumping show?

1Riders walk the course and memorize the track.
2Horse and rider warm up in a separate ring, called "training ring", then enter the arena to complete the course.
3Penalties are given for rails down, refusals or time faults.
4Clear rounds and fast pace create excitement for the audience.
Education content can be expanded at a later time.
CS

Concours de Saut

In Switzerland, show jumping competitions are often marked as “CS”. They can include regional or national classes depending on the organizer and level.

R / N

Competition levels

Regional and national categories help structure the sport. In Romandie, FER also supports championships, tours and cups across different categories.

4 pts

Common penalty

A rail knocked down or a refusal commonly adds faults. This makes the sport easy to follow: clear, accurate and fast rounds are rewarded.

Team

Behind the scenes

Every show depends on volunteers, sponsors, course builders, stewards, judges, riders, grooms and families. JumpLink makes that invisible ecosystem visible.

Problem & Solution

The sport is active, but the community feels scattered.

FER represents equestrian sport in Suisse Romande, while Swiss Equestrian provides national sport calendars and event information. JumpLink builds a bridge between official event data and everyday community needs.

📅

Fragmented information

Riders and fans often search across calendars, stable pages, social media posts and word of mouth to know what is happening.

🤝

Volunteer shortage

Horse shows need people for parking, bar, paddock, course crew, timing and hospitality, but recruitment is rarely centralized.

✨

Community energy

Parties, food stands and social evenings make events memorable, but this information is often hard to find before attending.

🔎

Mysterious sport

Show jumping has its own rhythm, codes and vocabulary. To newcomers, it can feel like a closed world; JumpLink makes it more welcoming, readable and easy to join.

The solution

A community-first event platform.

JumpLink Romandie presents show jumping events and adds the missing layers: volunteer sign-ups, sponsorship opportunities, social events and organizer visibility.

Show Jumping 101

Basic knowledge for newcomers.

The platform should also educate new people entering the sport, so they feel comfortable helping or attending.

What happens during a jumping show?

1Riders walk the course and memorize the track.
2Horse and rider warm up in a separate ring, called "training ring", then enter the arena to complete the course.
3Penalties are given for rails down, refusals or time faults.
4Clear rounds and fast pace create excitement for the audience.
Education content can be expanded at a later time.
CS

Concours de Saut

In Switzerland, show jumping competitions are often marked as “CS”. They can include regional or national classes depending on the organizer and level.

R / N

Competition levels

Regional and national categories help structure the sport. In Romandie, FER also supports championships, tours and cups across different categories.

4 pts

Common penalty

A rail knocked down or a refusal commonly adds faults. This makes the sport easy to follow: clear, accurate and fast rounds are rewarded.

Team

Behind the scenes

Every show depends on volunteers, sponsors, course builders, stewards, judges, riders, grooms and families. JumpLink makes that invisible ecosystem visible.
